# Sprint 2 Retrospective
**Campus Marketplace | 19 April 2026**

---

## Sprint Overview

Sprint 2 targeted a complete buyer experience for the Campus Marketplace — covering user dashboards and profiles for all roles (Student, Staff, Admin), real-time buyer-seller chat (US9), purchase and trade offer initiation (US8), drop-off slot booking (US10), seller ratings (US7), and the rating and review system (US16), while also resolving gaps carried over from Sprint 1.

It was a demanding sprint with a wide scope across multiple user stories and roles. The team got it done.

---

## What Went Well

**Team support when it mattered most:**
When the sprint got tough, the team showed up for each other. One member described it as going deep into the work and finding it harder and harder — but the team stayed in a meeting through the night to work through it together. That kind of support made the difference between a team and just a group of people on the same project.

**Technical problems were resolved:**
The app crashed at one point, which was a scary moment, but the team managed to diagnose and fix it without losing ground. No blockers remained unresolved by the end of the sprint.

**Smooth individual delivery:**
Several members reported that their assigned work — features like the chat UI, Firestore integration, slot booking, and dashboard builds — went smoothly on the technical side once they got into it. The planning and task breakdown from the Sprint 2 doc helped people work with clear direction.

**We delivered:**
Despite the breadth of this sprint (9 user stories, 8 new frontend routes, multiple roles), the team crossed the finish line. That's worth acknowledging.

---

## What Could Be Improved

**Communication needs to be more consistent:**
This came up from multiple team members independently. Messages were going unread, people were not always reachable when others needed input, and there was a general sense that the team was not fully in sync throughout the sprint. For a project this connected — where dashboards, chat, transactions, and ratings all depend on each other — poor communication creates blockers that slow everyone down.

**Some members disengaged once their part was done:**
There was frustration when members finished their own tasks and then went quiet, leaving others still working and struggling on calls alone. A sprint is not done until the whole team is done. Singling yourself out because your piece is complete is a pattern that erodes trust and puts unfair pressure on whoever is still working.

**Availability during collaborative sessions:**
People sleeping on calls and going offline while others were still working was a specific pain point raised. If the team commits to a working session, everyone should be present and active for the duration.

## Action Items for Sprint 3

- **Set a response time norm** — agree as a team on how quickly messages should be acknowledged (e.g. within 2 hours during working days).
- **Stay on calls until the session ends, not until your task ends** — if someone is still working, the team is still working.
- **Shared responsibility mindset** — if a teammate is struggling with something adjacent to your area, offer help rather than waiting to be asked.
